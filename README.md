# MIT 18.01 – Single Variable Calculus

Solutions to problem sets from **MIT OpenCourseWare 18.01: Single Variable Calculus** (Fall 2006).

> 📚 Course materials sourced from [MIT OCW](http://ocw.mit.edu)

---

## 📋 Overview

This repository contains my handwritten solutions to the problem sets assigned in MIT's 18.01 Single Variable Calculus course. The problems cover core topics in differential calculus, including:

- Derivatives — slope, velocity, and rate of change
- Limits and continuity
- Trigonometric limits and derivatives
- Differentiation formulas — product and quotient rules
- Chain rule and higher-order derivatives
- Implicit differentiation and inverse functions

### Part II — Problems
1. **Even/Odd Decomposition** — Express $(x-1)/(x+1)$ as the sum of an even and odd function.
2. **Sensitivity of Measurement** — Estimating distance on planet Quirk using GPS satellite geometry.
3. **Visibility Problem** — Finding the height at which an ant sees a cell tower atop a parabolic mound.
4. **Parabolic Mirrors** — Simmons §3.1 Problem 21.
5. **Rate of Change** — Average and instantaneous drain rate from a leaking water cooler.
6. **Limit Computations** — Various limit evaluations from Simmons §2.5.
7. **Product Rule Generalization** — Deriving and proving by induction the derivative of a product of *n* functions.
